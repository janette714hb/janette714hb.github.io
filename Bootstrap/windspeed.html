Summary: Latitude vs. Windspeed

According to "Sciencing", "Although there are other factors, a location’s latitude can give a hint as to whether its average daily wind speeds will be high or low. Worldwide, the area near the equator is notoriously calm, known colloquially as “the doldrums.” The nonequatorial tropics experience strong breezes known as the trade winds, but around 30 degrees latitude, there is often a deficit of wind." Typically, the equatorial region is known for calm trade winds. The higher wind speeds are higher latitudes is reflective of the seasonality of the data.
